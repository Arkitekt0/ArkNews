About Me

I am a data journalist dedicated to transforming complex datasets into clear, meaningful stories that help the public understand issues shaping their communities. My work combines statistical analysis, investigative reporting, and transparent methodology principles emphasized in leading data journalism research and practice.

My Mission

My mission is to use data responsibly and critically, recognizing that datasets are never neutral. I aim to reveal patterns, inequities, and insights that traditional reporting may overlook. I follow evidence based practices and prioritize clarity, accuracy, and public accountability in every story I produce.

How I Work

I follow established data journalism research: gathering data through public records, scraping, open data portals, and FOIA requests.

My Values

  • Transparency: I publish methodologies, sources, and limitations for every dataset.
  • Accuracy: I verify data quality, check for bias, and validate findings with experts.
  • Ethics: I consider the social impact of data stories and avoid misrepresenting statistics.
  • Accessibility: I present data visually and narratively so all readers can understand it.

Contact

I welcome questions, feedback, and data tips. Journalism is a collaborative process, and I encourage readers, researchers, and community members to reach out.
