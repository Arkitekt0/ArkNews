La Junta Native Set to Release Psychological Horror Video Game

Photo Credit: Gregory Montoya

LA JUNTA, Colorado — Gregory Montoya, a game developer originally from the area, is preparing to release the first chapter of his newest psychological horror video game, marking a major milestone in a journey shaped by resilience, creativity, and years of self-taught programming.

Montoya’s childhood in La Junta was marked by instability. With a father battling alcoholism, he moved frequently and attended nine different schools before reaching adulthood. Those early challenges forced him to adapt quickly and pushed him toward creative outlets that offered structure and expression. Game development eventually became the place where imagination, discipline, and problem-solving converged.

“Writing code felt like learning a new language,” he said, “one the computer could understand.”

By age 16, Montoya had already taught himself HTML, CSS, and Python, laying the groundwork for a future in programming. In 2004, he began building his first game, discovering a passion for crafting interactive worlds that allowed players to explore the environments he envisioned.

Breaking into the indie game industry came with its own obstacles. Competing for visibility in a crowded field pushed Montoya to focus on consistency, originality, and memorable experiences.

Data analysis has also become central to his work. He studies gameplay behavior to balance mechanics, pacing, and event timing, ensuring players experience tension and unpredictability.

Set for its first-chapter release in early December 2026, the game blends psychological horror with player choice, drawing inspiration from Silent Hill, Resident Evil, Fatal Frame, folklore, and urban legends.

“Make the game you’ve always wanted to play,” Montoya said.

A catastrophic setback nearly derailed the project when a hard drive failure erased the entire game. “Losing everything proved how much it meant to me,” he said.

As the release approaches, Montoya continues to inspire through persistence, ingenuity, and a commitment to pushing creative boundaries.
